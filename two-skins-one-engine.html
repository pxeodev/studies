Numbers first. Context second.

Each synthesis dimension gets a color-coded indicator bar.

18:50
Chat redesign done. Now the console.
coinrotator.app was serving raw JSON.
No UI. No console. Just a data dump.
An existing concept on Paper: Screener IDE.
Asset table with confidence dots. Regime badges. Terminal panel.
Tabs like screener.live, trend-flips.log, shumi.chat.
But then the question:
I feel like people don't really care about screener table views. We have almost no sticky users.
Screener tables are completely commoditized.
CoinGecko. CoinMarketCap. TradingView.
They all do it with more data, more assets, more features.
There's no reason to come back to yours for that.
What makes this different is the intelligence layer.
Confluence signals where RSI Bands HTF + KAMA Bands align.
Standalone win rate: 35-40%. Combined: 57-73%.
A table can't surface that.
So we killed the table.
Signal-first briefing.
Not "here's everything."
What changed since I last looked?

19:20
Two artboards on the canvas. Two completely different designs.
Same market data. Same intelligence layer.
Should they look identical?
I don't want identical. I want people to feel like they're wandering through a treasure hunt of useful environments.
Each surface is a room.
Shumi is the warm conversational guide.
CoinRotator is the cold precision instrument.
Same data. Same engine. Different costume.

20:20
Someone pulls up CryptoPanic for comparison.
Every headline, every language, every coin tag, promo popups, price tickers, trending carousels, signup CTAs — all competing simultaneously.
The design constitution makes that structurally impossible.
Tables are commoditized.
The edge is in the intelligence layer. Confluence signals, regime detection, synthesis across sources.
A table can't surface that.
"All caught up" is a feature.
Respecting the user's time increases return visits.
Twitter's infinite scroll keeps you trapped. This says: come back when something happens.
Surfaces are rooms, not clones.
Shared data shapes with themed skins create a constellation.
Each room has a reason to visit and a feeling you get when you're there.
Low intensity.
Pleasant to stay a while.
